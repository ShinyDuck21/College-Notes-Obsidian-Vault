## *McCulloch v. Maryland* (1819)
 - Second Bank of the United States, national bank first pushed by Hamilton
 - Operated in Maryland, Maryland taxed transactions in this bank
 - Constitutionality of Bank and constitutionality of state taxing federal government
 - Court Ruled "Necessary and Proper Clause" gave implied powers, Bank one such power - loose reading
	 - Also states cannot tax the federal government - 'the power to tax is the power to destroy'
- Vastly expanded federal government's powers
	- States v. federal (federalism), in favor of federal
