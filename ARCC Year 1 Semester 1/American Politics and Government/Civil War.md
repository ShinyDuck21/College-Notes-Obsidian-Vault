Republican Party v. Democratic Party 1854-Today

Republican Party formed largely in opposition to slavery, led by Lincoln

Democratic Party fractured, largely over slavery, pro-slavery in the South

Civil War start when Southern states seceded over fears of Lincoln exerting federal powers over states (especially abolishing slavery), Lincoln maintained states could not secede, war to stop them (House divided)

Issue publicly swung to slavery itself with Emancipation Proclamation - Declaration

Federal power grew greatly from the Civil War

Effects of Civil War (Balance of Power in Federalism)
 - Clarified no right to secede, no right to challenge legitimate federal laws
 - [[Amendment XIII]], [[Amendment XIV]], and [[Amendment XV]] are all Civil War Amendments
	 - Note: Congress shall have power to enforce... vs. Congress shall not
- Post-Civil War balance of power in federalism swung drastically to the federal government
 - Reconstruction - how to move forward
	 - Southern states reluctant
	 - Federal government grew in power to implement
- Southern states did not willingly secure rights after [[Amendment XIII]] and [[Amendment XIV]]
	- Representation v. voting, a problem - [[Amendment XV]]
- Such issues would remain prevalent moving forward
